Who is Mark Ryden

Photo: Ann Cutting, 2000

Mark Ryden is an American painter known for the genre of art that he dubbed "Pop Surrealism" in which he blended elements of popculture with surrealist aspects in order to comment on issues that he saw within society. "Ryden's vocabulary ranges from cryptic to cute, treading a fine line between nostalgic cliché and resonating archetypes. Seduced by his infinitely detailed and meticulously glazed surfaces, the viewer is confronted with the juxtaposition of childhood innocence and the mysterious recesses of the soul." (Ryden, 2014)

Accreditation

Since graduating from the Art Center college of Design in 1987, Ryden has been mostly known for his work with various musical artists and bands (See above, Ryden's work with Tyler the Creator). Ryden has worked with musicians such as Red Hot Chili Peppers, Michael Jackson, Jeff Beck, Jack off Jill and most notably Katy Perry. Ryden's most recent work was a collaboration with Barbie in 2022, adding his decrepit flair into the beloved character to really showcase his title as the "King of Surrealism", blending it with pop culture to add his own termed genre "Pop Surrealism"

Exhibitions and Books

Throughout his years as an artist, Ryden has been a focal point for his own art galleries and art books that the average consumer can obtain for their own coffee table.
